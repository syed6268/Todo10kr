# Cold Email Templates — Operator's Guide

A condensed reference of high-conversion cold-email patterns. Each entry
contains: when to use it, the structural skeleton, and a worked example.

---

## 1. The "Quick Question" Cold Email Template

**When to use:** First-touch with a stranger who is likely to ignore long
pitches. Lowest possible cognitive load.

**Skeleton:**
- Subject: `Quick question for {{first_name}}`
- Greeting: one line, name only
- Sentence 1: where you found them + specific thing you noticed
- Sentence 2: the single question
- Sentence 3: 15-minute call ask

**Example:**
```
Subject: Quick question for Garry

Hi Garry,

I came across your recent essay on agentic productivity tooling
and noticed you flagged orchestration as the next bottleneck.

Quick question: when you evaluate solo founders building in this
space, do you weigh shipped distribution more than depth of demo?

Would 15 minutes next week work to ask 2-3 follow-ups?

Thanks,
Mustafa
```

---

## 2. The "Third-Party Connection" Cold Email Template

**When to use:** You have a mutual contact (even a weak one). Leverages
social proof instantly.

**Skeleton:**
- Subject: `{{mutual}} suggested I reach out`
- Reference the mutual + the topic they pointed to
- One sentence on what you're building
- Specific ask anchored to their expertise

**Example:**
```
Subject: Anna suggested I reach out

Hi Priya,

Anna mentioned you've been pushing on cold-start retention for AI
copilots and thought we should connect.

I'm building Todo10kr, an agentic productivity scheduler, and we're
hitting the same wall: week-2 drop after the novelty fades. Would
love your take on the two interventions you tried at Linear.

Open to a 15-minute chat next week?

Best,
Mustafa
```

## 3. The "PAS" (Problem-Agitate-Solve) Cold Email Template

**When to use:** Selling a product directly. Works when the recipient
clearly owns the problem you solve.

**Skeleton:**
- Subject: `A faster way to {{outcome}}`
- Problem sentence (their POV)
- Agitate: what happens if they don't fix it
- Solve: your product in one line
- 15-minute demo ask

---

## 4. The "AIDA" (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action) Cold Email Template

**When to use:** You have one strong stat or surprising claim.

**Skeleton:**
- Subject: a number-driven hook
- Attention: the hook restated
- Interest: why it's true
- Desire: what they'd get
- Action: one CTA

---

## 5. The "Straight to Business" Cold Email Template

**When to use:** The recipient is senior, time-constrained, and known to
prefer no-preamble email.

**Skeleton:**
- Subject: `{{topic}} — 30s ask`
- One sentence: who you are
- One sentence: what you want
- One sentence: deadline if any

---

## 6. The "Paint a Picture" Cold Email Template

**When to use:** Vision-led outreach. Useful for early hires and design
partners.

**Skeleton:** open with a one-sentence future state, then show the gap
between today and that future, then invite them to help close it.

---

## 7. The "Something Useful" Cold Email Template

**When to use:** Reaching busy operators. Send them a relevant artifact
(a piece of analysis, a curated link list) and ask nothing.

**Skeleton:** "Saw your X — here's Y you might find useful. No need to
reply." Establishes credibility. Follow up a week later with a real ask.

---

## 8. The "Before-After-Bridge" (BAB) Cold Email Template

**When to use:** Product pitches where the transformation is concrete.

**Skeleton:**
- Before: their current pain
- After: the state they want
- Bridge: how your product gets them there in N steps

---

## 9. The "Mention a Competitor's Product" Cold Email Template

**When to use:** Selling to a buyer who has already evaluated alternatives.

**Skeleton:** name the competitor, acknowledge what it does well, position
your wedge as the part the competitor explicitly does not solve.

---

## 10. The "Praise-Picture-Push" (PPP) Cold Email Template

**When to use:** Outreach to investors and writers. Earns the right to ask.

**Skeleton:**
- Praise: a specific piece of their work and what you learned from it
- Picture: a one-sentence vision tied to that work
- Push: a 15-minute ask, with a concrete artifact you'll bring

---

## 11. The "Engagement Follow-Up" Cold Email Template

**When to use:** They liked / replied to a post but never converted.

**Skeleton:** reference the engagement, restate the value, single CTA.

---

## 12. The "Value Resource" Cold Email Template

**When to use:** When you've published something genuinely useful.

**Skeleton:** "Wrote this teardown of X — sending it because of your
work on Y. No need to reply unless useful." Builds list of warm contacts.

---

## 13. The "Value Proposition" Cold Email Template

**When to use:** The recipient is a target customer for your product.

**Skeleton:**
- One-sentence value prop (audience + outcome + differentiator)
- Two-sentence proof (numbers or named users)
- 15-minute demo ask

---

## Universal rules

- Subject line under 50 characters.
- Body under 120 words.
- One ask per email. Never two.
- Reference one specific, recent, verifiable thing about the recipient.
- Sign off with your name only; no slogans, no quotes, no banner.
- Send Tue-Thu, 7-9 AM recipient local time, for best open rates.
- Follow up exactly once after 4 business days, then drop it.
